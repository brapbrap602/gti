Chapter 903: Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony

It was Lu Yang’s first time seeing a bona fide sword spirit—a far cry from the counterfeit one residing in the Qingfeng Sword.

Yue Chuan’s sword spirit was undoubtedly of a high caliber; otherwise, Lu Yang wouldn’t have been able to perceive its uniqueness.

Yue Chuan smiled, unable to conceal his pride. “In the early days of my cultivation, I was fortunate to obtain a sword with a perfect spiritual nature. By nurturing it with my heart, I gradually cultivated A’Lan.”

His personal sword was called the Lanyan Sword, so the spirit named herself A’Lan.

To deepen their bond, he embraced A’Lan during the day and used sword-nurturing techniques to take care of her body at night. Countless people envied him.

The Everlasting Immortal Fairy couldn’t bear seeing Lu Yang being outshone. “As if no one else has a sword spirit! Xiao Yangzi, show them to me and broaden their horizons!”

Our Great Dou Dynasty is vast, rich with resources, and sparsely populated. As a pillar minister and future Dou Emperor, how could Xiao Yangzi lack a sword with a sword spirit?

The Everlasting Immortal Fairy felt that she was quite a competent emperor. I serve as Xiao Yangzi’s second nascent soul, and now I’m his sword spirit for appearances. Surely no emperor in history has been so attentive to a minister.

“No, no, please don’t! Immortal Fairy, just rest and recuperate in the Qingfeng Sword,” Lu Yang hastily dissuaded her. If she reveals herself, and others ask how I communicate and bond with my sword spirit, wouldn’t everything be exposed?

Mi Li and the others discussed Sword Dao with Lu Yang, feeling that they had met too late. His unique insights left them with a sudden sense of enlightenment.

A day passed in a flash.

“Let’s go. The Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony is about to begin.” Mi Li led the group downstairs.

The venue was temporarily constructed beside the Sword Tower, adjacent to the sword vault.

According to Mingtai, the Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony was the same as in previous years, divided into three segments. The first was the tower leader’s address. The second was the Sword Seeking segment itself; it wasn’t a competition of cultivation level but pure comprehension of the Sword Dao. The third was visiting the sword vault to resonate with spirit swords.

For attending sword cultivators, the second segment was the main draw. It was a chance to prove their Sword Dao comprehension to the world, representing absolute honor.

The Taciturn Daoist Priest had once claimed first at a Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony, then advanced rapidly and defeated the Sword Tower Leader, earning the title of number one in Sword Dao.

“Take my sword!”

“Kunlun Sword Art!”

“Heaven-Earth Single Sword!”

“Peacock Display Sword Art!”

Many sword cultivators seized the brief time before the ceremony to spar, swords clashing at the venue.

Some executed majestic strikes that could rip space, while others demonstrated sword techniques resembling peacock displays, hiding true attacks among countless feints. There was even an octopus that wielded eight swords and spun like an iron top, preventing anyone from approaching.

Previously, Lu Yang had only encountered rogue cultivators here and there, so it didn’t leave much of an impression on him. Now, with the Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony formally underway and sword cultivators from across the world gathered here, he was awed by the overwhelming sword intent in the air.

Aside from exceptions like the Taciturn Daoist Priest, most sword cultivators appeared aloof and taciturn because their hearts were wholly devoted to the sword, leaving no common ground with others.

At the Sword Tower, these sword cultivators finally found kindred spirits and came together in small groups. Most hadn’t known each other before, yet a few words engendered friendships.

This was another reason the Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony drew this much attention from sword cultivators.

Every sword cultivator possessed a unique intent. When these intents gathered, they would automatically coalesce into a soaring sword intent that dispersed the clouds without employing a single trace of power, revealing a clear sky above.

Lu Yang gazed at the blazing sun overhead. Couldn’t you at least leave me a patch of cloud? Back in my previous life, even our drill instructors during military training had the decency to pick a shaded spot for us to stand at attention.

If not for the inappropriate setting, Lu Yang would have executed One Sword Into Ten Thousand Swords to block out the sun.

The Sword Tower Leader stood at the highest point and addressed the crowd. Lu Yang couldn’t make out the words because Mingtai kept transmitting him auditory messages, introducing key sword cultivators to pay attention to.

“That tall, thin one in green is Duan Changqing from the Demon Region, a bamboo that turned into a demon spirit. He carved a section of his own body into a bamboo sword, using himself as a weapon.”

It was challenging for spirit plants to gain sentience, but once they did, they would be endowed with talents blessed by the heavens. Among spirit plants that became demon spirits, the one with the highest attainment was none other than the Epoch Immortal.

Though he was now reincarnated as Li Haoran and constantly on Su Yiren’s mind.

“That androgynous one is Kun Qiu of the Hundun Tribe. It’s said that he is highly regarded by their tribe’s Transcendent Tribulation ancestor and has been confirmed as the next tribe chieftain.”

Lu Yang stole a couple more glances at Kun Qiu. He had encountered Hundun Tribe prodigies in the Demon Region’s Creation Ancient Domain, but he was too busy fighting back then to note their race.

Kun Qiu wasn’t among those Hundun Tribe prodigies he had met. Lu Yang estimated that the other party’s age and cultivation level exceeded his own, though he couldn’t tell by how much.

Hundun’s gender was fluid, shifting between male and female, often appearing androgynous. This caused others to keep their distance.

Kun Qiu sensed Lu Yang’s gaze. When he turned to look at Lu Yang, the latter hurriedly snapped his head back.

“Kun Qiu, what’s wrong?” a large black dog beside him asked.

He was a prodigy from the Heavenly Dog Tribe and held a sword in his mouth. As he had practiced the sword in his original form since childhood, he still preferred it even after learning to shapeshift.

Kun Qiu muttered, “I felt someone watching me. It was probably my imagination.”

“Maybe they’re guessing your gender again.” The large black dog chuckled slyly. In the Demon Region, demons often speculated about Kun Qiu’s gender.

Kun Qiu shot the dog a sidelong glance. “Shut up. You’re drooling.”

Holding a sword in one’s mouth had one major drawback—the tendency to drool.

“That’s Xuan Xuan of the East Sea’s Xuanwu Tribe. They’re known for defense, yet he’s abandoned it and become obsessed with the sword. It’s said that even his peers’ turtle shells can’t withstand his blade.”

Xuan Xuan was dark and burly, carrying a broadsword on his back, with a small turtle he had drawn on the hilt.

At the Old Dragon Emperor’s birthday banquet, Lu Yang had seen the Xuanwu Tribe’s Transcendent Tribulation chieftain, but not its younger generation.

Seeing that the Sword Tower Leader was still speaking, Yun Mengmeng opened some oil paper, took out two pastries, and ate them stealthily. Lu Yang had bought them for her on the way to the Sword Tower.

Finally, the Sword Tower Leader announced the much-anticipated segment. “Next, we’ll proceed to the second segment—the Sword Seeking!”

Lu Yang revealed a confident yet restrained smile. He hoped to avoid fighting demons during the Sword Seeking segment. As Ao Ling and Jiang Lianyi’s senior brother, engaging in battle with younger demons would undermine my status.

Chapter 904: Sword Heart

After announcing the commencement of the second segment, the Sword Tower Leader continued, “A sword cultivator is not merely a cultivator who uses a sword as a weapon. If that were the case, who in this world wouldn’t be a sword cultivator?”

Lu Yang nodded inwardly. What the Sword Tower Leader said is right. Which cultivator hasn’t dreamed of being a sword immortal? They simply tried to use the sword as a weapon but found it unsuitable, so they gave up.

“Then does a sword cultivator refer to someone talented in Sword Dao? Not entirely either.

“A true sword cultivator not only possesses talent beyond ordinary people in Sword Dao, but also harbors a Sword Heart—that is, your attitude toward your sword!”

“There’s such a theory as Sword Heart?” Lu Yang was puzzled. I’ve never heard Shifu or Big Senior Sister mention it.

He felt that he really should attend more sword cultivator gatherings. I may not get to see Shifu even once a year, and after the Foundation Establishment Stage, Big Senior Sister has stopped teaching me sword techniques. As for the Everlasting Immortal Fairy, it goes without saying that she knows nothing about the Sword Dao.

“The Sword Seeking is divided into two parts: seeking the sword through the heart and seeking the sword through the intent.”

“Seeking the sword through the heart tests one’s Sword Heart.”

After hearing the Sword Tower Leader’s words, Lu Yang felt somewhat uneasy. If it were a competition of sword intent, I’d fear no one. But I’m hearing this “Sword Heart” thing for the first time. What should I do?

An array appeared beneath every sword cultivator’s feet, shooting a curtain of light upward and enveloping them.

“This is the Heart-Seeking Array. Within it, one cannot lie when answering any question concerning the ‘sword.’“

This was one of the Sword Tower’s heritage, an array that the Sword Tower Supreme Lord had unintentionally comprehended while ruminating on Sword Dao. It greatly aided one’s Sword Heart.

Lu Yang felt that this array shared some similarities with his Clear Heart True Nature Mantra, though it was more mysterious and profound.

The Heart-Seeking Array could distinguish truth from falsehood, but couldn’t initiate questions. The Sword Tower had twelve elders and one tower leader—thirteen in total—who personally stepped forward to test all the sword cultivators.

Among them, none drew more attention than the Sword Tower Leader. The sword cultivators he chose to test signified that he regarded them favorably to some extent.

The Sword Tower Leader first approached the bamboo demon spirit Duan Changqing. “Duan Changqing, do you cultivate the Heartful Sword Dao or the Heartless Sword Dao?”

“The Heartless Sword Dao.”

“If your sword breaks, would you be sad?”

Duan Changqing replied coldly, “If the sword breaks, I’ll just forge another. Why would I be sad?”

“Then do you like the Sword Dao?”

“Of course, I like it.”

The array beneath Duan Changqing’s feet blazed with glaring red light, indicating he lied.

The Sword Tower Leader spoke sternly, his eyes like swords seeming to pierce through Duan Changqing. “Say the truth.”

Unable to withstand the pressure, Duan Changqing confessed honestly. “I became a sword cultivator only because a sword cultivator’s offensive power is very strong.”

The Sword Tower Leader sighed. “Your sword was forged from part of your body. This certainly aids your mastery of the sword, but you feel no emotion for the Sword Dao. This will block your future path.”

He then approached his closed-left-gate disciple, Yue Chuan. The latter took out the Lanyan Sword, its sword spirit A’Lan nestled in his arms.

He knew Yue Chuan well, so there was no need to ask which Sword Dao he cultivated.

“Yue Chuan, can you live and die together with your sword?”

Looking at A’Lan in his arms, Yue Chuan declared firmly, “I can!”

“Then what is your relationship with your sword?”

Yue Chuan replied without hesitation, “I want to marry A’Lan!”

The Sword Tower Leader pressed a hand to his forehead. Asking Yue Chuan was just a formality. How could I not know what my eldest disciple is like? If it weren’t for the Great Xia’s laws not recognizing sword spirits as marriageable entities, the two would have wed long ago.

“Mingtai, can you live and die together with your sword?” The Sword Tower Leader’s tone was genial. In terms of seniority, unless the Sword Tower Supreme Lord revived, even if all past tower leaders were dug out, none would outrank Mingtai.

“I can!” Mingtai held the Hanguang Sword—the Sword Tower Supreme Lord’s personal sword and Mingtai’s original form. If anything happened to it, he wouldn’t survive.

“What is your relationship with your sword?”

“We are one entity.”

An uproar broke out from the crowd. The array showed no reaction, indicating that Mingtai hadn’t lied. Hearing his answer, countless cultivators felt ashamed. Although their feelings for their swords were sincere, compared to Mingtai’s, they still fell far short.

“Greetings, Tower Leader Guan.” Seeing him approach, Lu Yang hastily bowed.

The Sword Tower Leader’s name was Guan Yi.

“So you are Lu Yang? This is our first meeting.” The Sword Tower Leader smiled at Lu Yang.

Although he had conflicts with the Taciturn Daoist Priest, those were matters between seniors and unrelated to the younger generation. As the Sword Tower Leader, nothing delighted him more than seeing talented young sword cultivators grow.

With Lu Yang currently in the spotlight, the Sword Tower Leader’s words immediately attracted everyone’s attention.

“He really is Lu Yang.”

“I wonder what Lu Yang’s Sword Heart is like.”

“With Mingtai setting the bar, even if his Sword Heart is excellent, it can’t surpass Mingtai’s, right?”

Tower Leader Guan asked, “Your shifu cultivates the Heartless Sword Dao. What about you, Lu Yang? Do you cultivate the Heartful Sword Dao or the Heartless Sword Dao?”

Lu Yang hesitated. This was his first time hearing that the Sword Dao was divided into heartful and heartless paths. “May I first ask what the Heartful Sword Dao and the Heartless Sword Dao are?”

“Your shifu didn’t even tell you this?” Tower Leader Guan was somewhat displeased with the Taciturn Daoist Priest. Too irresponsible. Nevertheless, he began to explain.

“A sword cultivator certainly loves their sword, but that love can be categorized.

“The Heartful Sword Dao refers to treating the sword as a person and loving it like a beloved. Everyone in our Sword Tower cultivates this path.

“On the other hand, the Heartless Sword Dao refers to treating the sword as an object, simply loving it as a thing. The most famous practitioner of this path is your shifu.”

Lu Yang pondered over it. Although the Qingfeng Sword is a gift from Big Senior Sister and I like it, I ultimately regard it as an object, with no special feelings.

“In that case, I’m probably cultivating the Heartless Sword Dao.”

Hearing that, Tower Leader Guan was slightly unhappy. So it really is the case, huh? Lu Yang is such a promising seedling, yet that old Taciturn scoundrel has taught him the Heartless Sword Dao.

But then, the array beneath Lu Yang flashed red.

“Hmm? Did you just lie?” Tower Leader Guan’s face lit up with joy. Doesn’t this mean that Lu Yang is actually cultivating the Heartful Sword Dao?

“I lied?” Lu Yang was equally surprised. By Tower Leader Guan’s definition, I clearly fit the Heartless Sword Dao.

Tower Leader Guan guided him patiently and gently. “Do you like your sword?”

Lu Yang nodded. I do like the Qingfeng Sword.

“Do you treat your sword as a person?”

Lu Yang was about to shake his head when he suddenly realized the issue—there was a person inside the Qingfeng Sword!

Lu Yang could only nod again.

Tower Leader Guan clapped his hands, concluding on Lu Yang’s behalf. “Isn’t that it then?! You like your sword and treat it as a person—that is the Heartful Sword Dao!

“Suppose your sword broke or vanished. Would you be sad and weep bitterly?”

Lu Yang considered the scenario. If the Everlasting Immortal Fairy vanished, I would indeed be sad and probably even cry.

He could only nod again.

“See? This isn’t just the Heartful Sword Dao—you’ve reached the profound realm of entrusting your emotions to the sword!”

Inside the Qingfeng Sword, the Everlasting Immortal Fairy giggled foolishly. She was unsure why, but his answers made her elated.

“Then are you willing to live and die together with your sword?”

Within the Heartful Sword Dao, a small subset of sword cultivators, such as Yue Chuan and Mingtai, held extreme feelings for their swords, advocating ‘sword exists, I exist; sword perishes, I perish.’ Sword cultivators had historically revered such extreme emotions.

Hearing this question, Lu Yang’s expression turned peculiar, uncertain how to respond. “It’s not about living and dying together. Even if I die, my sword cannot die.”

Given the Everlasting Dao Fruit’s heaven-defying nature, it’ll be hard for the Everlasting Immortal Fairy to die even if she wants to.

Tower Leader Guan praised Lu Yang’s answer effusively. “Fantastic! Protecting your sword with your life, preventing your sword from suffering any harm, even at the expense of your life!”

Everyone turned their gazes to Lu Yang with respect. Only sword cultivators could grasp what his answer meant. This surpassed even “sword exists, I exist; sword perishes, I perish.”

“As expected of Lu Yang! With such devotion, which sword cultivator today can match him?”

“Earlier, I thought he only had talent in Sword Dao, with no feelings for his sword. I was mistaken!”

“No wonder he could perform One Sword Into Ten Thousand Swords and One Person Into Ten Thousand Persons at the Nascent Soul Stage. Apart from his talent in Sword Dao, his feelings for the sword surely played a pivotal role!”

Tower Leader Guan threw out his final question. “Then what is your relationship with your sword?”

Lu Yang felt that telling the truth would be embarrassing, but the array would detect falsehood, so he could only answer honestly. “The sword is the sovereign, I am the subject.”

Another uproar erupted from the crowd, even louder than when it was Mingtai’s turn. Faces were filled with utter disbelief as they stared at Lu Yang.

“Treating the sword as the sovereign—this is the legendary realm of Person and Sword as One and Body Moves With Sword!”

“No one in history has ever reached this level, right?!”

Sword cultivators were proud. No matter how well they treated their swords, they simply placed them on equal footing, never above themselves.

To set aside a sword cultivator’s pride for the sword—what a Sword Heart!

The more Tower Leader Guan looked at Lu Yang, the more pleasing to the eye he found him. The sword foremost, and the cultivator secondary; not even the Sword Tower Supreme Lord had cultivated the Heartful Sword Dao to this level!

Chapter 905: Sword Seeking Through the Intent

Mingtai gazed at Lu Yang with even more reverence. My Sword Heart is already rare in this world, yet Senior Brother Lu Yang’s feelings for his sword surpass even my own.

Throughout history—even in the enigmatic ancient era, and including the ancient Qin Sword Venerable—perhaps none had ever been like Senior Brother Lu Yang.

The only uncertainty is the Qilin Immortal. It’s said that he used a sword, but it is unknown whether he merely wielded it as a weapon or was a sword cultivator. If the Qilin Immortal were a sword cultivator, it would be unclear whose Sword Heart would be superior—his or Senior Brother Lu Yang’s.

Cultivating to reach immortality required unique insights into Sword Dao and Sword Heart.

“This Sword Heart is number one throughout the ages,” someone murmured, and many others immediately echoed.

“Lu Yang’s Sword Heart is number one throughout the ages!”

“Sword as the sovereign, Lu Yang as the subject—his Sword Heart is number one throughout the ages!”

Even the Sword Tower elders cast approving glances at Lu Yang, feeling that with his sword cultivation alone, he was worthy of having his name resound throughout the ages, serving as a role model for all future sword cultivators.

The slogans varied but shared the same essence, rising into a thunderous chorus like crashing waves. Embarrassed, Lu Yang lowered his head, curling his toes within his shoes.

It feels like everyone is calling me the Everlasting Immortal Fairy’s subject.

Lu Yang regretted attending the Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony. I shouldn’t have insisted on finding an excuse to travel; staying at the Dao-Seeking Sect would have been fine.

Yun Mengmeng didn’t understand Sword Dao well, but from everyone’s reactions, she could tell that Lu Yang’s Sword Heart was extraordinary.

She felt somewhat proud. “I’ve found a good teacher for Little Zhi. Granny even worried that I'd encounter accidents outside. With my luck, how could anything happen?”

With Lu Yang hailed as possessing the number one Sword Heart throughout the ages, any other sword cultivator’s answers truly couldn’t compare.

“See? It’s all thanks to this immortal that your Sword Heart is so amazing, Xiao Yangzi.”

Lu Yang ignored his sword spirit’s shameless claim.

“The sword seeking through the heart segment has concluded. I believe I don’t need to say whose Sword Heart is the most admirable.” Tower Leader Guan smiled, his gaze inadvertently falling on Lu Yang. If he could beat the Taciturn Daoist Priest, he would want to steal Lu Yang as his own disciple.

But come to think of it, it’s said that Yun Zhi cherishes this little junior brother dearly. Even if I could beat the Taciturn Daoist Priest, I probably can’t beat her.

Forget it. Lu Yang is fine staying in the Dao-Seeking Sect.

“Next, we’ll begin the sword seeking through the intent segment.”

The Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony was held once in a century. If it were one-on-one battles of sword techniques, those who had cultivated the longest would surely win, rendering the contest meaningless. Thus, the Sword Tower Supreme Lord proposed suppressing everyone’s cultivation to the same level, competing purely through sword intent.

Lu Yang felt a frightening pressure. It swept across the entire venue, suppressing everyone to the Golden Core Stage. Then, the earth trembled as eighteen platforms burst forth, encircling the Sword Tower.

Tower Leader Guan explained, “These eighteen platforms were forged by the first tower leader. You only need to place a strand of sword qi on them, and it will manifest as a sword-intent avatar, which you can control in battle.”

“These are the legendary sword intent arena platforms?” The sword cultivators’ eyes gleamed at the sight of the eighteen platforms that were as smooth as mirrors.

These were supreme treasures left by the Sword Tower Supreme Lord. Prolonged cultivation atop them could hone one’s sword intent, making it sharper.

It was Lu Yang’s first time seeing these, but he could grasp their significance by hearing the discussions in his surroundings.

After drawing lots, Lu Yang was assigned to the second group and would have to wait for the first group’s matches to finish before it was his turn.

He recalled the key cultivators Mingtai had mentioned—among the first group was Xuan Xuan of the Xuanwu Tribe.

Xuan Xuan carried a heavy, broad black sword on his back, with a small turtle drawn on its hilt. When he walked, it felt as though the ground quaked and the mountains shook.

Lu Yang also recognized Xuan Xuan’s opponent. It was the octopus with eight swords coiled in its tentacles.

“Wu’er, I didn’t expect to meet you here.” Xuan Xuan grinned, revealing eight white teeth.

Both he and the octopus were sword cultivators from the East Sea. They had heard of each other’s reputation before, but never exchanged blows.

“Xuan Xuan, they all say my Sword Dao is inferior to yours. I never found the chance, but today, I’ll dispel that rumor!”

“What rumor? They’re simply speaking the truth. How could you compare to me?”

Wu’er attacked first. Tentacles coiled around eight swords, spinning wildly without an opening. Those who had sparred with him before the ceremony couldn’t strike his main body.

Xuan Xuan yelled and hefted his black sword, swinging it at Wu’er.

Clang!

Such a strike would trouble any sword cultivator, but upon reaching Wu’er, the sword was deflected by the rapidly spinning eight swords.

“There’s indeed some substance.” Xuan Xuan grew intrigued. Sword cultivators were known for offense, yet Wu’er adopted the opposite approach—defense.

“Isn’t it just spinning? Anyone can do that!” Xuan Xuan reverted to his original form—a black turtle with a serpent’s body.

He flipped over, resting on his raised shell. Head tucked into the shell, he gripped the sword hilt with his mouth and used his tail to push off the ground, sending himself whirling.

As his tail struck the ground with increasing speed and force, Xuan Xuan spun increasingly faster until his head and tail blurred into one. Using his tail to accelerate and steer, he charged at Wu’er, knocking him over.

Wu’er also grew furious. This move has never failed. It’s my first time getting knocked down.

“You think I fear you?!”

The two seafolk spun and collided like iron tops. Whoever was sent flying would muster strength to slam back into their opponent.

Lu Yang watched Xuan Xuan and Wu’er with admiration, surprised to witness such fusion battle techniques here. But these two seafolk were even more impressive; they could spin individually, which was far more convenient.

Observing that the two iron tops were unlikely to decide a victor anytime soon, Lu Yang circled the Sword Tower to observe other arena platforms.

“Little Feng! Why...why are you here?!” One sword cultivator looked terrified upon seeing his opponent’s face and name.

The one named Little Feng smiled stiffly. “Brother Lin, I told you we’d meet again. Did you think I wouldn’t be able to find you just because you changed your name? I knew you’d definitely attend this centennial Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony!

“Blood debts must be repaid in blood. Let’s settle this with our life and death on this arena platform today!”

“Second Junior Brother, who Little Junior Sister likes is her own affair. Don’t let the result of our duel decide her marriage!”

“Enough talk! Let’s fight!”

“So what if you’re still alive? You’re nothing but worthless trash without a spirit root. There’s no way you can beat me!”

“Kekeke. You’ll probably never understand the meaning of ‘break to rebuild.’”

“...” Lu Yang. Sword Tower, you didn’t rig the draw for the matchups, did you?

Chapter 906: A Tenacious Opponent

Tower Leader Guan fell into deep contemplation at the sight.

How can we possibly investigate every sword cultivator’s background? The Sword Tower lacks an intelligence department like the Dao-Seeking Sect’s Great Mission Hall; we can’t check them all. Besides, such situations had never arisen in any past Sword-Seeking Grand Ceremony.

Strange. A Sword Tower elder sensed something unusual about this year’s ceremony and secretly transmitted an auditory message to Tower Leader Guan. “Tower Leader, this may be an ill omen. Should we make some preparations?”

Tower Leader Guan nodded, agreeing with the elder’s assessment. Matters at this year’s ceremony have exceeded the Sword Tower’s capabilities. “It’s indeed an ill omen. Notify the authorities first and have them send more personnel, lest they lack manpower to make arrests.”

The Sword Tower isn’t one of the Five Great Immortal Sects; we lack enforcement authority.

~~~

Aside from the numerous irreconcilable conflicts among the respective sword cultivators, the first group’s matches had nothing else noteworthy.

Xuan Xuan and Wu’er’s iron top duel attracted many eyeballs. As the two seafolk clashed to their limits, Wu’er activated his hidden bloodline, and an ancestor’s phantom appeared overhead, granting him new strength and giving him the upper hand. Next, Xuan Xuan also activated his hidden bloodline, summoning his ancestor’s phantom, turning the tide, and gaining the advantage.

In the end, Xuan Xuan’s last strike prevailed, securing victory.

With that, the first group’s matches concluded.

Since everyone had fought using sword-intent avatars, even if they killed their opponent, it merely caused their sword intent to collapse and didn’t pose a danger to their lives.

Of course, if they sought revenge in private afterward, that couldn’t be helped.

Compared to the first group, the second group’s matches drew more interest. It featured Mingtai and Lu Yang, two widely acclaimed young Sword Dao rising stars. People had only heard legends, and this would be their first glimpse of them in action.

However, the most conspicuous was Yue Chuan, who brought his sword spirit A’Lan directly onto the arena. As sword spirits were considered part of the sword, this didn’t violate the rules.

Lu Yang didn’t let his sword spirit—the Everlasting Immortal Fairy—make an appearance. If she does, forget the sword cultivators participating this year, even all sword cultivators from past ceremonies combined wouldn’t be enough to take her on.

I, Lu Yang, am upright and aboveboard; I don’t rely on superficial external forces.

Lu Yang’s opponent was a somewhat oddly dressed cultivator, pale-faced and frail-looking, carrying an ice sword engraved with snowflake patterns.

He remembered Mingtai mentioning this person, a cultivator named Xue Shilou who was somewhat famous in the Extreme North. To be noted by Mingtai, he naturally had merits. However, compared to Lu Yang, he ultimately fell far short.

“Poor Xue Shilou, meeting Lu Yang in the first match.”

“Can’t help it; he’s too unlucky.”

“I wonder how much power he could force Lu Yang to display.”

“I hope the fight lasts longer. The more of Lu Yang’s trump cards he reveals, the better for us.”

Despite hearing the murmurs below the platform, Xue Shilou’s gaze remained exceptionally resolved. So what if Lu Yang’s talent is great? I can bridge the gap through effort. My decades of sword practice—tears, sweat, and scars—are countless.

Throughout my journey, how many strong foes have I faced? How many people said I couldn’t win? Yet reality slapped them in the face and proved them all wrong.

On the arena platform, dozens of blows were exchanged in a flash. Living up to his reputation as a Sword Dao genius honed by years in the Extreme North, Xue Shilou’s sword intent style carried a tinge of chill and decisiveness.

It was Lu Yang’s first time fighting with a sword-intent avatar, and he found it quite novel.

Now that I’ve experienced what it feels like to fight a sword-intent avatar, it’s time to get serious.

As Lu Yang’s sword moves changed, Xue Shilou felt mounting pressure, as if Lu Yang wielded not a sword but a giant hammer capable of shaking heaven and earth. Each clash numbed his wrist.

Is this Lu Yang’s true strength? Had he merely been warming up just now?

Cold sweat formed on Xue Shilou’s forehead. Both of us are using sword-intent avatars, which have nothing to do with our cultivation. This also signifies that Lu Yang’s sword intent far surpasses mine.

Lu Yang’s moves were heavy and swift, seemingly piercing Xue Shilou’s moves. Each sword strike was aimed at his weakness, forcing him to passively defend without the chance to counter.

The spectating sword cultivators could also tell that Xue Shilou’s resistance was futile and that his defeat was a matter of time. However, none of them could imagine faring better than Xue Shilou if they were in his place.

“How about you just admit defeat?” Lu Yang suggested sincerely, hoping to finish early and observe other matches before they ended.

“No!” Xue Shilou said firmly. Though he was at a disadvantage, his gaze remained resolute, unshaken by the gulf in their abilities.

Lu Yang was stunned, then realized his attitude was wrong. As a sword cultivator, one should fight to the last moment; how could I suggest surrender? My words were an insult and a slap to Xue Shilou’s face.

He said earnestly, “Apologies. In return, I’ll use my full power to defeat you.”

Sensing Lu Yang’s increasingly heavy and rapid assaults, Xue Shilou sweated profusely, feeling like his blood was boiling.

With this move, he had once pierced through a xuanwu at the Apex Transcendent Tribulation Stage with a single sword strike. Despite the Xuanwu being renowned for defense, it was as fragile as paper before this technique!

Xuan Xuan felt that even their Transcendent Tribulation old ancestor had to evade this strike, lest the sword shatter their vital defenses.

Before this move, the sword-qi seafolk demons scattered into lawless sword qi, absorbed by the Chengying Sword’s emitted aura before they could even touch the sword.

Clang!

All the sword qi was devoured by the Chengying Sword. Only the Hanguang Sword, which was also an immortal sword, remained unaffected, clashing with the Chengying Sword.

The collision of the two great immortal swords spanned the past and present, shaking the earth. The whole sword vault teetered, and the Sword Tower’s foundation destabilized, threatening to collapse at any moment.

The two unparalleled sword cultivators ignored this as they unleashed their Sword Dao, seeking to overwhelm the other party. As their Sword Dao fields expanded, neither appeared as majestic as everyone had imagined.

The Sword Tower Supreme Lord’s field teemed with ghost phantoms. These were all powerful foes she had slain, replicated by her Soul Incipient Dao Fruit and restored within the field.

Liu Ningxuan’s field resembled the world’s end. It was dark red, with the sun sinking and stars shattering. Any living being entering would inexplicably die, as if struck by inevitable karmic death.

Even with Lu Yang’s talent in Sword Dao, he couldn’t understand these two Sword Dao fields, let alone the others.

Much less needed to be said for the Everlasting Immortal Fairy; all her understanding of Sword Dao came from Lu Yang.

The Sword Dao fields expanded rapidly, scouring every inch of space. Space itself grew unstable, cracks continuously forming as if on the verge of shattering!

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Under the collision of the Sword Dao fields, the Sword Tower above finally succumbed, collapsing into ruins that threatened to bury the sword vault.

Yet the Sword Dao fields hurled the Sword Tower’s debris aside, leaving the two unaffected.

Both panted, staring intently at each other. They were supreme Sword Dao experts of their respective eras. Despite having met comparable opponents, they had never encountered one to rival them in Sword Dao alone.

“Immortal Fairy, who will win?” Lu Yang asked anxiously.

Although the Everlasting Immortal Fairy was unfamiliar with strength, she could discern differences in strength.

“Tsk, with how they’re fighting, it will probably take at least a day or two for an outcome. However, that Sword Tower girl’s foundation is too weak; she can’t last that long.”

“Ah?” Lu Yang grew concerned for the Sword Tower Supreme Lord on Mingtai’s behalf.

Liu Ningxuan had noticed this too. Although he was a sword spirit and couldn’t revert to human form, his foundation was solid, unlike the Sword Tower Supreme Lord, who was like a flickering candle soon to be snuffed out.

Then the Everlasting Immortal Fairy added, “But with a third party’s intervention, Liu Ningxuan could be dealt with quickly.”

“A third party’s intervention?”

Just as Lu Yang was puzzled about where a third party could come from, he saw a familiar figure soar up high, joining the Sword Tower Supreme Lord.

“Fellow Daoist, I’m here to help you.”

“Yun Mengmeng?!”

“Miss Yun?”

Recognizing that figure, Lu Yang was stunned. Yun Mengmeng has been freeloading off me on meals all this time. How did she suddenly become a third party capable of fighting alongside the Sword Tower Supreme Lord?

Tower Leader Guan and the others hadn’t expected Lu Yang’s acquaintance to be so formidable.

“Her Golden Core cultivation was a disguise!” Lu Yang figured it out. This Yun Mengmeng hasn’t been honest, huh?

“Wait, Immortal Fairy, why didn’t you tell me?” I can’t see through her, but surely the Immortal Fairy could!

He frantically recalled their conversations. I don’t think I’ve said anything terribly rude to Senior Yun Mengmeng.

The only potential problem is her good sister's identity. I had apparently offered to teach her swordplay.

For some reason, Lu Yang grew restless as a sense of foreboding swelled within him.

“I saw you were getting along well with her. I was worried that if I had told you her cultivation, you'd hold back and spoil the fun.” With that, the Everlasting Immortal Fairy made a face at him and turned away, ignoring him.

“Who are you?!” Liu Ningxuan was startled by Yun Mengmeng’s appearance. He and the Sword Tower Supreme Lord were in a delicate balance; the slightest shift could tip the scales of victory.

Yun Mengmeng initially wanted to introduce herself, but reconsidered. There’s already an immortal named Yun Mengmeng, but I’m not an immortal; if I use that name, won't it bring disgrace to the name?

“My name is Yun Zhi.”

Lu Yang’s legs turned to jelly, and he almost wanted to drop to his knees.

I’m toast. The Immortal Fairy doomed me.
