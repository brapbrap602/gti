Chapter 1780: The Gods Return

Even when achieving true godhood, survival in this darkness was incredibly difficult, let alone for that Eternal World. Like a candle extinguished, it could not resist at all.

Fortunately, Zhang Fan's clone, holding the Transformation of Truth and Falsehood, could turn things around with a single thought, reigniting the dying embers. But... all he could do was this.

Just like the Mountains and Seas. The new Ark, placed with high hopes by the God-Conqueror Guests, fell into the same endless cycle of reincarnation shortly after setting sail. Moreover, its fate was far more cruel than that of the Mountains and Seas!

The Mountains and Seas, in their cyclical reincarnation, could last for hundreds of billions of years. Infinite variables, myriad life forms evolving. However, the new Ark of the God-Conqueror Guests... could only last for a few breaths.

Each time they returned to the moment they left the Mountains and Seas, they were like flickering candles in the wind, on the verge of being extinguished. Moments later, they would be completely extinguished, only to be reset by [Truth and Falsehood], returning to the moment of departure.

This cycle repeated. If, in the reincarnation of the Mountains and Seas, the God-Conqueror Guests could "lie flat" for hundreds of billions of years, then on the new Ark, they were trapped in a continuous cycle of death. There was no hope, no future. Only endless purgatory.

In the cold and silent darkness, there was no concept of time. But from Zhang Fan's perspective, the candlelight had flickered millions of times.

Starting from the millionth cycle, Zhang Fan had sensed a faint thought of "seeking release" from his clone. But Zhang Fan's reply was, "No."

In the darkness, one could not avoid the fate of decay. Fortunately, the candlelight on the new Ark continuously flickered, serving as a warm anchor, greatly slowing down Zhang Fan's decay.

"Don't blame me; this is the only way to try and survive." Amidst the struggle in the darkness, Zhang Fan's mind occasionally flashed with such thoughts. Initially, he felt some guilt, but soon his resolve became firm.

"If one wants to escape fate, someone must sacrifice."

"Don't worry, just wait for me until I can withstand this darkness like a True God..."

With the candlelight as his guide, Zhang Fan struggled to survive. Gradually, he gained new insights into the surrounding darkness. This was the most primitive, fundamental, and ultimate state of the universe. The place where all things were born and perished.

"Even a True God cannot alter this cycle of life and death. They can only use the divine ability of [Truth and Falsehood] to maintain their existence."

"Gods are like this, let alone others?"

"All beings and things in the Mountains and Seas, and even the God-Conqueror Guests, are merely derivatives of a god's power. Without the protection of Truth and Falsehood, they naturally cannot last for even a moment."

"..."

The candlelight only served to slow down the decay, not prevent it. Zhang Fan once again smelled the scent of decay upon himself.

"Saints, Mountains and Seas, God-Conqueror Guests, the Star."

"All were fleeting passersby on a larger stage, each taking their turn in a lively performance."

"But there is always an end."

"The only one who can reappear after perishing is god."

"Because that is the original anchor point of [Truth and Falsehood]."

Countless voices whispered and echoed in his mind. Zhang Fan, however, was no longer panicked as he was at first. He gazed at the distant candlelight, striving to maintain the calmness of his thoughts.

"Just like the anchor at Year 1 for me."

"Regardless of what happens in the reincarnation, with a single thought, everything will return to the predetermined moment."

"God is worthy of being the true master of the Great Dao of Truth and Falsehood. Their use of [Truth and Falsehood] is even superior to mine. Without initiating a thought, as long as everything perishes and meets the conditions for god's demise, it can be triggered."

"Returning to the time when the [True God] still existed, the original anchor point."

"And the true environment of the universe is the absolute basis for the plan to be carried out."

"Even gods themselves need Truth and Falsehood to maintain their existence; everything born from gods naturally cannot escape this fate."

"No matter what, everything will perish in Truth and Falsehood, leading to the return of the True God."

"My previous instances of True Return were merely creating small cycles within the larger cycle."

"..."

The cacophony of voices grew louder, making it difficult for Zhang Fan to think steadily. But a sense of despair instinctively arose in his heart. He seemed to be facing a dead end. The only way to break free from the cycle set by the True God was to render Truth and Falsehood ineffective and nullify the True God's anchor point.

But Zhang Fan now knew that even Truth and Falsehood itself could not escape the changes of Truth and Falsehood. He was merely a temporary holder of the Great Dao of Truth and Falsehood after the True God's fall. How could he rewrite the rules of [Truth and Falsehood]?

"The only thing that can be done is to repeatedly perform True Return, to prolong survival, just like..." Zhang Fan looked again at the candlelight not far away.

"Similar paths, leading to the same outcome, no difference."

Billions of years of time, reduced to a few breaths. At first glance, the two were worlds apart. But if the number of reincarnation cycles was infinite, then there was no difference between them. Both were merely pathetic beings struggling endlessly.

"It's meaningless..."

The scent of decay grew stronger, and Zhang Fan's will was gradually overwhelmed by the increasingly fervent voices.

"Fine, you may go." Granting his clone Xiao Fan's desire for "release."

When the tiny candlelight, which had flickered for hundreds of millions of cycles, finally extinguished, the destruction Zhang Fan faced became even more rapid.

Instinctively, he wanted to perform True Return, but myriad images flashed through his mind – all the things he, or rather his original body, had experienced. Zhang Fan felt a sense of weariness.

"It's meaningless..."

Despair, absolute nothingness, enveloped Zhang Fan, making him completely disregard the crisis of impending demise. He accepted death with peace and tranquility. His focus was no longer on himself, but on contemplating the master of [Truth and Falsehood], the former True God.

Suddenly, Zhang Fan felt something was subtly amiss.

"Since everything is predetermined, why did He go to such great lengths to capture me, an outsider?"

Zhang Fan suddenly felt bewildered. Then, in an instant, he understood: "An outsider possesses a characteristic that even gods lack: the ability to maintain self-change amidst True Return."

"Gods, like all beings in the Mountains and Seas, try to break this endless cycle of reincarnation. Hence, my arrival."

"His purpose..." Zhang Fan's thoughts raced. He recalled his original body scattering clones everywhere, seeking variables. He recalled the incomplete parts of his memories.

"So that's how it is."

Zhang Fan should have felt anger. But at this moment, he was unusually calm.

"If one wants to escape fate, someone must sacrifice." He muttered this sentence repeatedly. Previously, he had said it to his clone, Xiao Fan. This time, it was to himself.

For Zhang Fan's nature, making such a decision was extremely difficult. It was only after much struggle, with furrowed brows finally relaxing, that he spoke.

"Then, I shall descend into the ultimate end of divinity."

Zhang Fan now had no fear of death. Because he knew that after his demise, his original body would undoubtedly reappear. The soaring decay completely engulfed Zhang Fan. The clamor of voices abruptly ceased.

A god was reborn. A god had returned.

Chapter 1781: The End

The Time of God's Appearance

As the starlight was devoured, all external sights and sounds vanished. True, pure darkness and silence completely enveloped Zhang Fan. Apart from Zhang Fan, there was nothing else. Even the concept of time ceased to exist. After a long period of absolute stillness, faint murmuring sounds began to echo in Zhang Fan's ears, like thunder against the profound quietness of his surroundings.

"This is..." Zhang Fan found the voice strangely familiar but couldn't recall it for a moment. Although only an instant had passed since the return of all things to divinity, it felt like an eternity had passed in Zhang Fan's eyes. Even his memories began to decay and blur. Instinctively, he tried to listen carefully. It was like a sudden cicada's chirp, followed by a chorus of thousands. What was mere whispering one moment became a grand, magnificent symphony the next.

"Xuan Yuanhong, Fate Slayer Master, Old Fisherman..." Familiar yet strange names surfaced in Zhang Fan's mind, causing him to hesitate. Zhang Fan no longer remembered who these people were. But their desperate shouts, seeming incredibly urgent, were telling Zhang Fan something. Sensing their emotions, Zhang Fan tried to calm himself, to exclude the influence of other distracting sounds. But it was to no avail. Soon, these familiar voices were drowned out by the incoming tide. New voices began to emerge. Both familiar and strange. So familiar that Zhang Fan could instantly recall their names, but so strange that his mind held no memory of them. Zhang Fan had a vague premonition that something was wrong.

"Am I... decaying?" In this moment, Zhang Fan was filled with profound confusion and bewilderment. He suddenly recalled his goal: to slay gods. His ambition from years ago was still vivid. After proudly devouring the Mountains and Seas and annihilating the Star, his realm, strength, and state of mind had all reached their peak. He was fully prepared to face the rumored fallen True God. But he never imagined... No True God appeared. Instead, he was met with endless solitude and darkness. For an instant, then an eternity. His body and consciousness began to decay and wither. And within him, all that he had previously consumed began to resurrect. This was entirely different from what he had expected. As if deceived, a surge of malevolence erupted in Zhang Fan's heart.

"Get out!" "Get out!" he roared in extreme anger. But even he couldn't hear his own voice. The dark void remained eternally unchanged, like cold seawater silently engulfing Zhang Fan. In this instant, Zhang Fan felt the bone-chilling cold within this darkness. He felt himself suddenly becoming weak, the ground he relied on for survival rapidly disappearing. Instinctively, he tried to gasp for air, but he couldn't reverse this trend. The sounds in his ears grew louder and louder, like dark clouds obscuring the sky. Zhang Fan felt like a dying behemoth, lying on the ground, devoid of all strength, unable to struggle, letting the scorching sun bake him, his body decay, and insects feast upon him.

"I am dying." This realization flashed through his mind. Then, immense unwillingness surged within Zhang Fan. "I worked tirelessly, growing from an insignificant clone step by step to this point. I devoured my original body, consumed the Mountains and Seas, destroyed the solitary Star. I nearly attained godhood!" "How could I be dying so meaninglessly?" Zhang Fan angrily waved his hand, trying to drive away the insects swarming around his body. But as he raised his arm, it also became a breeding ground for insects. Not only did the situation not improve, but the surrounding voices grew even louder, like a roaring flood. Despite his millions of grievances, Zhang Fan felt his own existence becoming increasingly fragile, drawing closer to death. In a daze, he seemed to hear a terrifying chewing sound. It was as if a colossal being in the darkness was silently consuming him. Compared to the minuscule insects, its feeding was slow and elegant, seemingly savoring the fall of this "new god" with a hint of mockery, quietly appreciating it. Like being doused with a basin of ice water in winter, Zhang Fan's boundless rage instantly extinguished, leaving only fear. At this moment, no thought of resistance arose in his mind. The only thing that remained was the desperate struggle to escape its maw. But its feeding was unquestionable, an inescapable destiny. Zhang Fan could only watch in absolute clarity as every inch of his body was gnawed, crushed, swallowed, and then digested. All that he had consumed was lost in this invisible digestion. Ultimately, only Zhang Fan himself remained. In the next moment, even Zhang Fan himself was about to be completely consumed. With no external restraints or burdens, Zhang Fan suddenly felt a resurgence of hope for survival. He had forgotten many things, virtually everything. But one thing he remembered: "Truth..." "Truth and Falsehood..." Like a flickering lamp in the darkness, even if wavering, it briefly illuminated Zhang Fan's lost thoughts. "The traces of... Truth and Falsehood?" He vaguely recalled that in a certain world, Truth and Falsehood was about to be born. It was only a hair's breadth away from completion but could never succeed due to its location within the Mountains and Seas. In the process of proving his Dao, Zhang Fan had not devoured that world. He had merely allowed it to change. "It's still there!" As if seeing hope, Zhang Fan's remaining essence was ignited by the flames. He struggled to open his eyes, trying to find the only possible refuge in the boundless darkness. "It's there!" His memories resurfaced, and Zhang Fan quickly located his target. An Eternal World was floating silently, as if it had been there since its inception. Unchanged through the ages. The silent feeding in the darkness regarded him as nothing, as if he were a final lifeline. Zhang Fan's remaining divine sense resisted the devouring force from outside and quickly reached the Eternal World. Although it was only a small corner, it brought Zhang Fan an unprecedented sense of security. All external threats vanished instantly, and Zhang Fan managed to survive. The darkness receded, and time regained its meaning. Zhang Fan gradually regained his composure. The experience he had just endured was like a nightmare. Even now, Zhang Fan could not fully comprehend what had happened. But the familiar aura of Truth and Falsehood emanating from this Eternal World brought him a sense of peace. His rationality gradually returned. "It actually succeeded in nurturing?" This was the first normal thought that arose in Zhang Fan's mind.
